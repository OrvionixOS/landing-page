The Problem

Your listing may look fine while Google quietly buries it.

Most ranking drops are not caused by one dramatic failure. They come from several small signals stacking against you: a risky business name, a stale photo library, missing categories, weak Q&A, review silence, or competitors becoming more active.

The audit is built to find the exact pattern. Every issue is scored High, Medium, or Low, then translated into an action plan you can use immediately.

High Severity

Keyword-stuffed business names

Since 2024, Google has been punishing names padded with city terms, service keywords, pipes, and slogans. If your name is stuffed, cleanup becomes the first fix.

High Severity

Wrong primary category

The primary category controls what searches your listing is eligible for. A broad or inaccurate category can suppress an otherwise healthy profile.

Medium Severity

Photo, post, and Q&A gaps

Google rewards active listings. No posts in 90 days, old photos, and an empty Q&A section all make the business look less current than competitors.

Medium Severity

Diversity Update risk

If the GBP links to the same page that ranks organically, Google may suppress one of those placements. We check for this before recommending a website-link change.

The New Protocol
What happens in every audit

Find the root cause before touching the listing.

We start by pulling the live listing, the top three local-pack competitors, and the baseline signals before changes are made. The goal is not a generic SEO report. It is a ranked recovery plan.

  • Every issue logged and scored High, Medium, or Low.
  • Top three competitors checked against the same signals.
  • Business name, category, activity, NAP, and website-link risks reviewed.
  • Client deliverable includes the Loom, action plan, photo brief, post calendar, and review templates where included by tier.
Root Cause 01

Business name penalty

A sudden drop often points to a stuffed GBP name. Any city, service term, descriptor, slogan, or separator gets flagged immediately.

Root Cause 02

Activity stagnation

Gradual decline usually means the listing stopped posting, uploading photos, earning reviews, or answering questions while competitors stayed active.

Root Cause 03

Competitor improvement

Sometimes your listing did not break. Someone else improved faster. The gap table shows exactly where they pulled ahead.

Root Cause 04

NAP or entity confusion

Old addresses, tracking numbers, alternate names, and directory mismatches can make Google unsure which business entity to trust.

Root Cause 05

Diversity Update conflict

Since August 2024, a strong GBP and organic result can conflict when both point to the same page. We check whether a secondary page would protect visibility.
